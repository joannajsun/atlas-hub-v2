# Product Strategy
**Atlas Hub 2.0 — Last-Mile Delivery Operations Platform**
*May 2026 | Joanna Sun*

---

## The Strategy

The white-glove delivery market is underserved because every existing tool solves a different slice — truck tracking, route sequencing, enterprise orchestration — without anyone connecting the dots between *what happened on a delivery* and *what should change tomorrow*. Atlas enters at the intelligence layer, initially for regional carriers too small for Bringg and too sophisticated for Onfleet. We build trust with dispatch leads through daily operational value, generate data that becomes defensible over time, and convert that data advantage into a moat before enterprise players push downmarket.

---

## Strategic Bets

**Bet 1: The intelligence gap is real and durable**
No competitor will build exception intelligence for regional white-glove carriers in the next 18–24 months. Samsara and Verizon are moving upmarket. Onfleet is food-first. Bringg requires $200K+ contracts. DispatchTrack has the vertical focus but isn't investing aggressively in analytics. The risk: a well-funded startup enters the same space. Mitigation: move fast on data collection — our moat builds with every exception logged — and lock in customer relationships before the category is publicly validated.

**Bet 2: Dispatch leads are the wedge, VPs are the economic buyers**
Bottom-up adoption converts faster than top-down sales at this market size. If Maria's day is better, she champions Atlas to David. Direct VP outbound is a long, expensive cycle. The implication: build for Maria first, build for David second, and never let those priorities flip.

**Bet 3: Data is the moat, not features**
Exception patterns, FACR benchmarks, address-level flags — these get more valuable the longer a carrier uses Atlas. A carrier 12 months in has history that a new tool can't replicate. Every exception logged is a moat contribution. This is why exception log completeness is a P0 metric from day one.

**Bet 4: White-glove delivery is the beachhead**
The same intelligence layer applies to other high-touch, high-failure-cost delivery categories (medical equipment, custom furniture, exercise equipment installation). We start here because failure costs are highest ($200–500/failed delivery vs. $2–5 for parcel), the buyer is clearest, and the tooling gap is largest.

## Positioning

> "Atlas Hub is the operational intelligence platform for regional white-glove carriers. Unlike route optimization tools that stop at 'which order to visit stops,' or telematics tools that only know where your trucks are, Atlas answers the question that actually costs money: why are deliveries failing, and what changes tomorrow to prevent them?"

## Go-to-Market

**Phase 1**: Founder-led direct sales to 10–20 ICP targets. Source: furniture carrier associations (NHFA), regional logistics directories, Wayfair/Ashley carrier networks. Goal: 5 paying customers with deep founder relationships.

**Phase 2**: Informal referral relationships with happy customers. One or two partnership conversations with carrier associations.

**Phase 3**: Formal channel deal. Target: 10+ inbound leads/quarter from channel.

**Pricing**: $800/mo (Starter, 1–20 trucks), $1,400/mo (Growth, 21–60 trucks), $2,000/mo (Scale, 61–100 trucks + multi-warehouse). Annual contracts; monthly available at +15%.
