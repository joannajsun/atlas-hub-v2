# Customer Journey Map
**Atlas Hub 2.0 — Last-Mile Delivery Operations Platform**
*May 2026 | Joanna Sun*

---

## Two Intersecting Journeys

**Buyer journey** (David) — how Atlas gets purchased
**User journey** (Maria + Marcus) — how the product gets used after purchase

Winning the buyer without winning the user means we get paid once and churn at renewal. The user journey determines whether David sees ROI at 90 days.

---

## Buyer Journey (David, VP of Operations)

**Unaware**: David is managing with Tuesday spreadsheets and uncomfortable SLA calls. He doesn't know a purpose-built tool exists.

**Aware**: A peer carrier mentions Atlas, or David sees content that names his exact problem ("why your SLA conversations are hard").

**Consideration**: Takes a demo. His questions: "How do I know this will improve our numbers? How hard is adoption? What does migration look like?" We counter with: ROI calculator, reference call, 30-day trial.

**Decision**: Contract negotiation. He needs annual vs monthly resolved, and someone to answer the IT security question. We commit to a 14-day onboarding timeline.

**Critical insight**: David approves the purchase but Maria's behavior determines whether he renews. If the product is great and Maria doesn't use it, David sees nothing at the 90-day review.

## User Journey — Maria's Morning (before vs. after)

| Time | Without Atlas | With Atlas |
|------|--------------|------------|
| 5:45am | Checks TMS, makes mental notes on risky stops | Opens Morning Brief — unconfirmed count, vehicle alerts, exception baseline. Done in 10 min. |
| 6:00–6:45am | Calls customers she's worried about based on memory and gut | Works from the unconfirmed list. Has access notes already logged. |
| Route exception mid-day | Driver calls. Maria logs in TMS (maybe). Reschedule is manual. | Exception notification in < 30s. Two taps to reschedule. Customer auto-notified. |
| 4pm | GM asks why Route 7 had 3 failures. She knows why but can't prove it. | Pulls exception history for Route 7. Shows it. |

**The moment Maria becomes a champion**: The first morning she flags an unconfirmed stop, calls the customer, confirms delivery — and that stop completes successfully. She'll tell someone about it.

## User Journey — Marcus's Day

**Before warehouse departure**: PDF manifest on his phone, incomplete notes, finds out about elevator outage from another driver via text.

**With Atlas**: Opens confirmation checklist. Sees prior exception flag on stop 3 ("building elevator out of service — 3 prior logs"). Asks dispatch to call ahead before leaving.

**Exception mid-route**: Customer not home. Previously: calls dispatch, remembers to write it up at EOD (20 minutes). With Atlas: three taps, exception logged with reason code, dispatch notified, done.

**End of week**: Opens self-view, sees FACR: 94%, rank 2nd on team. Takes pride in that number.

## Moments That Make or Break Adoption

| Moment | What breaks it | What makes it |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Maria's first Morning Brief | Loads slowly or shows wrong data — she doesn't come back | Loads fast, data is accurate, she tells someone |
| Marcus's first exception log | Too many taps, crashes on 3G — he calls dispatch instead | First log in < 60 seconds, confirmation on screen |
| 30-day FACR trend view | Chart is empty or obviously wrong — trust broken | Trend line matches what Maria remembers happening |
| David's 90-day review | FACR flat, no visible improvement — renewal at risk | Trend up, exception recurrence down, partner data ready for Ashley call |
