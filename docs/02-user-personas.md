# User Personas
**Atlas Hub 2.0 — Last-Mile Delivery Operations Platform**
*May 2026 | Joanna Sun*

---

## Persona 1 — Maria, Dispatch Lead (Primary User)

Maria, 36. Dispatch manager at a Bay Area regional carrier. 8 years in logistics.

> *"I know something's going to go wrong today. I just don't know which stop yet."*

She manages ~180 white-glove deliveries a day across furniture and appliance routes. She's not a tech skeptic — she uses Samsara, she knows Onfleet. She's skeptical of tools that create more work than they save.

Her morning looks like this: arrives at 5:45am, checks her TMS, makes mental notes about risky stops, texts or calls customers she's worried about based on memory and gut, sends routes out at 6:45am, and then spends the next 8 hours reacting. At 4pm her GM asks why Route 7 had 3 failures. She knows why — she just can't prove it.

**What she needs**: Know which stops are at risk before the truck leaves. Log exceptions without adding 20 minutes of paperwork. Have data she can actually show her GM.

**Current tools**: Proprietary TMS, Google Sheets, iMessage threads with drivers, Samsara for truck locations.

**What a good day looks like**: She spends the morning proactively fixing problems that haven't happened yet. Fewer than 2 failures. She leaves before 5pm.

## Persona 2 — Marcus, Crew Lead / Senior Driver (Field User)

Marcus, 31. Crew lead on furniture and appliance routes. 5 years in white-glove delivery.

> *"I just need to know what I'm walking into before I get there."*

He takes pride in his FACR — 94%, one of the best on the team. His failures almost always come from bad information: a customer who was at work all day, an apartment with a broken elevator nobody flagged, a sectional that doesn't fit through a 28" doorway. He's frustrated when those failures land on his record.

**What he needs**: Accurate access notes and confirmed customer status before he leaves the warehouse. A fast way to log exceptions from his phone. Visibility into his own performance numbers.

**Current tools**: PDF manifests on his phone (notes usually incomplete), phone calls to dispatch when something goes wrong.

## Persona 3 — David, VP of Operations (Economic Buyer)

David, 44. VP Ops at a multi-market regional carrier. He owns the P&L and the retailer relationships.

He doesn't touch individual routes. He manages with weekly Excel reports that Maria's team assembles by hand — usually arriving Tuesday for the Monday review. He's going into Ashley Furniture SLA calls without clean data. It's uncomfortable.

**What he needs**: FACR and on-time rates across all markets without waiting for a spreadsheet. Data to defend (or acknowledge) SLA performance with retail partners.

**He's the buyer.** Maria's adoption determines whether he renews.
