# Stakeholder Map
**Atlas Hub 2.0 — Last-Mile Delivery Operations Platform**
*May 2026 | Joanna Sun*

---

## Within the Carrier Organization

**Maria (Dispatch Lead)** — primary daily user. Uses Atlas every morning. Her adoption determines whether David sees ROI at renewal. If she doesn't love it, the product fails regardless of how good the executive dashboard is.

**Marcus (Crew Lead / Driver)** — mobile user, exception logger. His consistency determines data quality. Doesn't care about dashboards — needs a fast, reliable mobile experience and some form of personal feedback (his FACR).

**David (VP/GM of Operations)** — economic buyer. Approves the contract. Needs FACR data for retailer SLA conversations. His involvement is weekly/monthly, not daily.

**Operations Analyst** — may handle route data import, driver account setup. A bottleneck during onboarding if not supported. Needs clear documentation.

**IT / Systems Admin** — periodic touchpoint. Reviews data security and SSO requirements. Needs a clear answer on PII handling and data isolation.

**HR** — aware FACR data may surface in performance conversations. May raise privacy concerns about individual driver monitoring. Mitigation: anonymization option on crew leaderboard, position Atlas as coaching tool not surveillance.

---

## Influence Map

```
David (signs contract) ←── Maria (daily champion)
                                    │
                            trains + manages
                                    ↓
                          Drivers / Crew Leads
                          (exception logging)
                                    │
                                    ↓
                             Data quality
                                    │
                                    ↓
                           FACR improvement
                                    │
                                    ↓
                       David renews / expands
```

The cascade is real: David buys, Maria drives adoption, Marcus logs data, data quality drives FACR improvement, FACR improvement is what David sees at renewal. A break at any point in that chain ends in churn.

## External Stakeholders

**Retail partners (Ashley, Wayfair, Best Buy)** — not Atlas customers in v1, but downstream beneficiaries. If Atlas helps a carrier improve SLA performance with Ashley, Ashley rewards the carrier with more volume. This creates an indirect incentive to invest in Atlas. Phase 4 option: retailer portal where carriers share performance data voluntarily.

**Carrier associations (NHFA)** — primary GTM channel. Member list = ICP prospect list. Conference presence and newsletter are awareness channels.

**Competitors** — watch DispatchTrack for any analytics roadmap announcements. Watch for YC-batch or well-funded startup entries into the white-glove intelligence space.
