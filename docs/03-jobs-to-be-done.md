# Jobs To Be Done
**Atlas Hub 2.0 — Last-Mile Delivery Operations Platform**
*May 2026 | Joanna Sun*

---

## Framework

Jobs-to-be-Done captures the real motivation behind a user action — not "I want a dashboard" but "I want to walk into my 6am shift knowing what's at risk so I don't get blindsided." Features are hired to do jobs.

Format: **"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."**

---

## Maria — Dispatch Lead

**Morning Risk Scan**
> When I arrive at the warehouse before routes leave, I want to see which stops are most likely to fail today, so I can intervene before the truck leaves and turn a probable failure into a successful delivery.

**Exception Response**
> When a delivery failure is reported by a driver mid-route, I want to log the reason and trigger a reschedule immediately, so I can close the loop in under 5 minutes without losing track of what happened.

**Pattern Recognition**
> When I notice the same type of failure happening repeatedly on a specific route or with a specific retail partner, I want to see aggregated exception data, so I can fix the root cause instead of firefighting each instance.

**Crew Coaching**
> When I need to have a performance conversation with an underperforming driver, I want to show them their FACR trend and specific failure instances, so the conversation is grounded in data rather than my impressions.

---

## Marcus — Crew Lead

**Pre-Route Briefing**
> When I'm loading my truck in the morning, I want to see today's stops with accurate customer confirmations and access notes, so I don't discover a problem at the customer's front door.

**Fast Exception Logging**
> When a delivery fails, I want to log the reason in under 60 seconds on my phone, so dispatch knows immediately and I don't have to remember it for end-of-day paperwork.

---

## David — VP of Operations

**Retailer SLA Defense**
> When a retail partner challenges our SLA performance, I want to pull accurate delivery data segmented by partner, so I can either acknowledge the issue with a remediation plan or show that failures were caused by their own order data — not us.
